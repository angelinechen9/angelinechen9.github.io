Gradually, as I acquired more skills, coding became a process of breaking up the intended outcome into component modules. Many students began to realize that computer science is both applicable and enjoyable, and most wanted to learn more. Multiple students thanked me for successfully introducing them to code. Specifically, I recall teaching a lady who had no prior coding experience how to build a website to promote her online business. She thanked me for being patient and nonjudgmental when updating her on new technology.

Guiding students through the process of learning computer science, I encouraged them to explore their interests and to acquire skills that benefit themselves and society as a whole. As a mentor, I have positively influenced my students and favorably changed their perspectives about computer science.
